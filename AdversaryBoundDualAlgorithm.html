aPaths in the space of Gram matrices: A tightened adversary bound-universal query algorithm dualityVery early version, full of typosFeedback: fi1w+RhoPaths - e-mail a - outlook.com This note discusses quantum query algorithms in discrete time1, developing an adversary lower bound and dual universal query algorithm for state conversion problems with non-commuting, non-unitary oracle operators. New resultsI focus on discrete time. Compared to the most recent previous papers by
(Belovs, 2015; Lee et al., 2011)
, the approach given here1. doesn't require the computational model to allow reverse queries,2. can be generalized to arbitrary quantum channels as oracles, in particular faulty oracles2. A workable lower bound method could help understand the extent to which a quantum computer can obtain speedups even if errors are not corrected, and be practically relevant. Caveats of the method presented here are that a perfect no-op must remain possible, I have not yet applied the method to a concrete algorithmic problem, and it may be hard to use in practice. This will be presented in a future version.I also present a simple universal query algorithm that, compared to the one given in the papers above,1. removes a factor of (ln1/)(ln1/) in the asymptotic runtime3, 2. doesn't use phase detection,3. and has a simple geometric interpretation as traversing a straight line between shifted initial and final reduced density operators.In a continuous-time model,
(Brandeho & Roland, 2015)
 presented an adiabatic algorithm with the same asymptotic complexity.4 By an argument by Kothari given in
(Belovs, 2015)
, a further asymptotic tightening is not possible in this framework. ExpositionI also hope that this text has expository value as it1. gives a unified development of several ideas around the adversary bound using a new approach,2. gives geometric meanings to both adversary bound (as the inverse maximal speed in a space of reduced density operators) and dual algorithm (as a straight line in a space of reduced density operators).To follow this text, one only needs basic knowledge in quantum physics including reduced density operators, purifications and their local equivalence.
